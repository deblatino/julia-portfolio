A Boutique Hospitality Template for Squarespace

Sample: California Cabin Retreat

One Template. Infinite Luxury Properties.

Julia is a system for independent hosts with boutique properties. Any intimate, curated stay that deserves a premium digital presence. The template works because it focuses on what guests actually want: the story, the atmosphere, the experience. Not a generic property listing.

Works for: Mountain cabins, Beach bungalows, Glamping sites, Winter retreats, Any boutique property.

Luxury Without a Designer Budget

Independent hosts want their properties to feel as premium as Airbnb Luxe. But they cannot hire a designer. They need a template that feels custom, works beautifully, and requires zero code to maintain.

Guests Book Experiences. Hosts Tell Stories.

Before designing solutions, I had to understand who would actually use this template. Two distinct users. Two distinct needs.

The Guest's Perspective

Guests are not searching for a "room with amenities." They are searching for an experience. They want to understand the story of the property—the aesthetic, the vibe, the feeling of stepping into something carefully curated.

They want to trust it. They want to imagine themselves there. And they want clear, trustworthy paths to book.

The Host's Perspective

Hosts want their property to feel as premium as Airbnb Luxe, but they cannot hire a designer full-time. They need a template that looks custom, feels bespoke, and is easy enough for them to update themselves without touching code.

They are caught between wanting something beautiful and needing something practical.

The Core Insight: Guests book experiences. Hosts need help telling the story. Every design decision that followed was about bridging that gap.

Two Visions. One Hybrid Choice.

Rather than presenting one option, I created two directional prototypes to explore different interpretations of the brief. This gave the client agency and visibility into the design thinking.

The Platform Did not Have What We Needed. So We Built It.

Here is where the real design work started. Squarespace 7.1 is powerful, but it has limits. No custom code. No filtering for galleries. Limited typography control. No icon libraries without licensing fees.

Most designers would see these as constraints to work around. I reframed them as design opportunities—moments where creative thinking could produce better solutions than custom code ever would.

The Mindset Shift: Every platform limitation became a question: "What elegant solution does this force us to discover?"

When Galleries Can't Filter, They Become Journeys

When galleries could not be filtered, I created curated slideshows organized by space: Main Cabin, Living Areas, Outdoor Spaces. Guests browse at their own pace. It feels like a guided tour, not an image dump.

From Icons to Premium Photography

Custom icon libraries cost money and add complexity for hosts. Instead, I created photo tiles where hosts upload photos of their actual amenities. More luxurious. More personal. More authentic.
